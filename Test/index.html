Industry Influences

How we got here from there

This is important to understand: Computer technology has been built up over the years as a product of two primary influences:

  • The nature of logic, mathematics, and the physical world — electricity, magnetism, optics, mechanics, materials, and so on.
  • The choices that key industry players have made in deciding how to take advantage of the behavior of logic, mathematics, and the physical world. For example, one key choice was the decision to use the binary number system as the basis for computer logic.

These choices could have been completely different than they were, and still produced devices that we could use instead of the computers we have now. But as each design decision was made (usually for good reason, sometimes not), the industry was focused more and more toward the resulting technology that we have today.

What is a Computer? Why Do We Care?

A comparison

Consider a typical tool like a hammer. Hammers are designed with only one purpose: to hit things. (Some hammers can also pry things apart.)

A computer, on the other hand, is the first tool in history that’s designed to do an unlimited number of things with information. (And it can also use information to control actual devices and tools, including hammers.)

Breaking it down

Every computer has two parts:

  • Hardware (stuff you can touch)
    • CPU & memory
    • Input devices
    • Output devices
    • Storage
    • Communication
    • Specialized hardware
  • Software (stuff you can't touch)
    • Information
      • Data (ingredients)
      • Instructions (recipes)
    • Protocols, conventions, and standards
